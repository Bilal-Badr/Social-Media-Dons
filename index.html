Your Questions Answered

'Social media dons' refers to the pervasive and often unnoticed control social media platforms exert over our lives, leading to addictive behaviors and negative consequences. It's about recognizing the subtle ways these platforms can dominate our time, attention, and emotional well-being.

Common signs include: constantly thinking about social media when not using it, feeling restless or irritable when unable to use it, needing to use it more and more to achieve the same satisfaction, neglecting other important areas of life, and repeatedly trying to cut down on use without success.

Not necessarily. While digital detox periods can be highly beneficial, our goal is to help you develop a balanced and mindful relationship with social media. This often involves setting boundaries, reducing usage, and finding healthier alternatives, rather than complete abstinence.

The timeline varies greatly for each individual, depending on the severity of the addiction and commitment to change. It's a journey that requires patience, self-compassion, and consistent effort. Our programs provide ongoing support to help you through this process.
